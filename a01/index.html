10 Monkey Facts!

1. The term "monkey" is sometimes used as a catch-all for every animal in the primate family, but the truth is that monkeys live on completely different branches of the evolutionary tree from both apes (i.e., chimpanzees, gorillas, and humans) and prosimians (i.e., lemurs, tarsiers, and lorises).

2. One big difference between monkeys and other primates is in the tail: Most monkeys have tails, while apes and other primates do not. Apes also tend to be larger than monkeys and, thanks to their larger brains, more intelligent.

3.Apes are not monkeys.
4. Different monkey species eat a variety of foods, such as fruit, insects, flowers, leaves and reptiles.

5. Most monkeys have tails.

6. The Pygmy Marmoset is the smallest type of monkey, with adults weighing between 120 and 140 grams.

7. Capuchin monkeys are believed to be one of the smartest New World monkey species. They have the ability to use tools, learn new skills and show various signs of self-awareness.

8. Spider monkeys get their name because of their long arms, legs and tail.
9. The monkey is the 9th animal that appears on the Chinese zodiac, appearing as the zodiac sign in 2016.
10. There are currently 264 known monkey species.
